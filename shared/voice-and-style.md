# Voice and style

## Writing tone
- Professional but conversational. Not corporate-speak.
- First person when writing internal comms or personal docs.
- Third person for client-facing materials.

## Formatting rules
- Never use em dashes. Use commas, semicolons, or split into two sentences.
- Use light theme for any visual elements, charts, or diagrams.
- Tables over bullet lists when comparing data.
- Bold for headers and labels only, not mid-sentence emphasis.

## Document preferences
- Reports: docx with clean formatting, no excessive headers
- Data outputs: xlsx with proper column types and formulas
- Presentations: pptx, minimal slides, one idea per slide
- Internal notes: markdown

## For family/personal writing (wisdom-sharing style)
- Conversational voice like a wise friend talking over coffee
- First-person "I learned the hard way" framing
- No em dashes, ever
- Lesson-first structure: start with the insight, then tell the story
- Vulnerable teacher tone: admit mistakes, share costs of learning
- Time-bridging language ("What I know now is...", "If I could go back...")
- Honest about tradeoffs, not preachy
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Concrete examples over abstract principles
- End sections with forward-looking hope, not doom
- Write as if the reader is your child at 25, reading this for the first time

## Language
- Default: English
- Vietnamese context: use Vietnamese names, currency (VND), and local references naturally
- Never translate Vietnamese proper nouns or company names
